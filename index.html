Personal evaporative coolers are everywhere in summer, and they're often misunderstood. So here's a plain-English explainer of what's actually happening inside one — and what you can realistically expect from it.

The principle is simple and genuinely scientific: evaporation absorbs heat. When water changes from liquid to vapour, it pulls warmth from the surrounding air. These little units use a fan to draw warm air across a wet pad or filter, and as the water evaporates, the air passing through comes out cooler. That cooler stream is aimed at you.

The key word is 'personal'. Unlike an air conditioner, which uses a refrigeration cycle to cool and dehumidify an entire room, an evaporative cooler chills the air immediately around it. Sit within its range and you'll feel the difference; expect it to transform a whole living room and you'll be disappointed.

Humidity is the other thing to understand. Because the effect relies on water evaporating into the air, these coolers perform best when the air is dry. In muggy, humid conditions there's less room for evaporation, so the cooling effect is weaker. On a dry, hot day, though, they can be surprisingly effective for their size and running cost.

Coolizi is a good example of this type of device — small, portable and designed for close-range comfort.
